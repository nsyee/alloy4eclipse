Syntax for appended facts

An appended fact is written by placing constraints in curly braces immediately after a sig statement. They can make a model clearer by logically grouping related constraints together. 

  sig name {
    //fields of the signature
  }{
   //appended fact constraints
  }

Any sig mentioned in the appended fact has an implied "all n:name |" at its beginning, and and implied "n." in front of it ( "name" is the name of the sig the fact is appended to). Thus 

  foo.bar

is really saying 

  all n:name | (n.foo).(n.bar)

Consequently, appended fact can be more concise (and thus clearer) than the equivalent non-appended fact. However, there is also a danger in forgetting that every sig mentioned in an appended fact is prepended with "n.".
